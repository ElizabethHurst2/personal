SMALL CHANGE MARKET

A annual fundraiser collectively organized by clay club to raise money for community and mutual aid. To find out about markets, follow @smallchangemarket on instagram.

Clay club members learned make a unique slipcast cup each year by etching into plaster casts or marbling colored pigment into slip. We sell hot drinks and whiskey kindly donated by local businesses out of the cups, and have raised over $10,000 dollars between our 2019, 2022, 2023 and 2024 markets. Brooklyn, 2019-present.

2024

The 2024 market included canvas and leather bags from Carrion Luggage Company, pill advent calendars from Xinan Helen Ran, a variety of print, ceramics and jewelry artists, homemade coquito, live airbrushing, the return of PROP HOUSE, beautful candles and brushes from Boy Boy Blackbear, swag from Plaza Proletaria, RealCompost T-shirts and prints from Compost for President, and more. .

2023

The 2023 fundraiser included a variety of art and ceramics artists, jewelry made from Riis beach shells by Mads Moore and Donnie Zakheim, pocket planters by Serena Seshadri and Em Eason, and custom airbrushing from Sarah Lammer.

2022

The 2022 fundraiser included a variety of art and ceramics artists, prints by Serena Seshadri, Em Eason, and Patrick Costello, music curation by Evelyn Manlove, and custom airbrushing from Sarah Lammer.

2019

The 2019 market included a variety of handmade crafts, art by Patrick Costello and Tim Fite, custom airbrushing from Sarah Lammer, and live analog video art experiences by Allen Riley.
